**Episode 13: Turning Point**

\
After we finished our sweets, we exchanged a few easy words and went our separate ways, and I made my way home alone.

The night breeze was pleasant against my skin.

*(Surprisingly... I actually relaxed a little.)*

Time with Shiori was, strangely, not uncomfortable.\
If anything, it had been just the smallest bit easier.\
Because the loneliness of Honoka not being there had eased, just a fraction.

Not that I've gone off Honoka. That's not it at all.\
Something like that couldn't happen — not in a million years.\
If anything, the more I'm conscious of her absence, the more I'm reminded of just how much I love her.

But right now, I was afraid of being swept away by those feelings.

Without Honoka I'm lonely. I want to see her. I want to hear her voice.\
Having nothing but those feelings swelling up inside me, that tightness in my chest that won't let go — honestly, it hurts.

So the relief of having those feelings ease, even just while I was with Shiori — I was grateful for it.

And yet.

I took out my phone on impulse and stared at the screen.\
No notifications from Honoka.

...Am I sitting here waiting for her to message me?

Any other time, I would have been the one to reach out first without a second thought.\
But now I was hesitating to send anything myself.

I decided to stop being possessive, to keep my distance — and here I am, still with my head full of nothing but Honoka.\
My heart hasn't changed at all.

I let out a slow breath and put the phone back in my pocket.

Then, out of nowhere, it buzzed.

For just a moment, I let myself hope. *Maybe it's Honoka—*

I checked the screen quickly.

But what was there was a message from Shiori.

【Thanks for today. I was so happy to get to talk with you, Nanase-san.】

I stared at that short message for a while.\
Right — we'd exchanged contact details at the café.

I hesitated for a moment over whether to reply.\
But ignoring it would be strange, so I sent back just a brief 【Same here.】\
The phone buzzed again almost immediately.

【About the aquarium I mentioned at the café — have a think about whether you'd like to go.】

*(...the aquarium.)*

It had been a topic I'd half-let slide past me at the café, but Shiori was genuinely inviting me.

*(Should I...go?)*

There was still a faint resistance in me, to going somewhere with anyone who wasn't Honoka.

*(But if it's Shiori...)*

It hadn't even been that long since we started talking, and yet I found that I'd let my guard down around her.\
That was probably because she'd listened to my problems and been so considerate of me. So I found myself wanting to take her up on the invitation.

And besides —

*(I want to properly build connections with people outside of Honoka.)*

Maybe going to an aquarium, just as a first step, would be all right.\
I looked at the message thread with Shiori and let that thought settle.
