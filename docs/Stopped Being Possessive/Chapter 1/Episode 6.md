**Episode 6: Conflict**

\
When I got home, the only sound in the quiet room was my own footsteps.\
I dropped my bag on the bed without thinking and took out my phone.

Honoka's messages hadn't increased. Just that one.

【Yuna, are you not coming to get me today?】

Such a small thing to say. And yet it squeezed my chest so hard.\
Any other day I'd have replied straight away — *I'm coming* — and gone to her without a second thought.\
Today I hadn't done that.

*"Try giving yourself a little more room."*

Shiori's words kept turning over in my head.\
If keeping my distance meant Honoka wouldn't have to suffer —

But was that really the right thing?\
I love Honoka.\
I need her, from somewhere deep inside me, in a way I can't do anything about.

"...Is it because I love her that I keep my distance?"

I don't want to be apart from her.\
If I could, I'd stay right beside her always.\
I want her field of vision to hold nothing but me.

But if that was becoming a burden to her —

I sank into the bed, phone still gripped in both hands, and let out a long, slow breath.\
My fingers moved on their own, opening her contact page.

"...No."

If I messaged her now, it would be the same as always.\
I have to hold on.\
If I really love her, I have to think properly about what's good for Honoka.

I shut my eyes tight.

But even with them closed, her face rose up behind them.\
That troubled smile. The faintly lonely line of her profile.\
Yesterday after school — that split-second of hesitation when I pulled her hand too hard.

*— Was I hurting her?*

I didn't want to think about it. But the question stuck in my head and wouldn't let go.\
I fell back onto the bed, still holding the phone.

"...I'm being ridiculous."

I was the one who decided to keep my distance. And now I can't stand it.\
One small step apart and I already feel this unmoored.\
This desperate to see her.

I looked at the screen again.\
Her name, glowing there.\
If I wanted to, I could send it right now — *I want to see you after all* — just like that.

I couldn't send it.

"...Shiori."

Her words rang through my head again.

*"I see you, Nanase-san. I really do."*

Those eyes.\
Something composed in them. Something searching. Like she was looking into a part of me I hadn't shown anyone.\
What is Shiori thinking?\
Was that kindness — just kindness? Or something else entirely?

But there's no point turning that over.\
Right now, I have to think about Honoka.

I switched off the screen and pressed my face into the pillow.

What kind of face will Honoka make tomorrow?\
What did she think, when I didn't come to get her?\
Did she miss me, even a little?\
Or... did she feel relieved?

My chest went tight.

"...I knew it. I can't do without Honoka."

No matter how kind Shiori's words are.\
No matter how much she tells me to give myself room.\
In the end, the only thing inside me is how I feel about Honoka.

So I'll keep my distance — for Honoka's sake.

Whether that's right or not, I don't know.
