**Episode 2: Anxiety**

\
Class had started. I sat there, gazing absently out the window.

The teacher's voice wasn't reaching me. The only thing in my head was Honoka — who'd been right beside me until just a moment ago.

*— I wonder if she's talking to someone right now.*

Just the thought made my chest go restless.

The image came to me so easily: Honoka, smiling, talking with someone. Honoka, at someone else's side. The thought refused to leave.

I ground my back teeth.

*There's no point thinking about this.*

"Hey, Nanase-san."

A small voice from beside me.

I glanced over. Shiori was looking my way, smiling softly.

"...What?"\
"If you keep staring out the window like that, the teacher's going to get mad at you."

A teasing tone. But her expression was gentle somehow.

"I don't care."\
"Heh. You really are easy to read, Nanase-san."

She laughed, quiet and small.

"What do you mean?"\
"Just that you care about Shiraishi-san a lot."

My heart lurched for a moment at those words.

"...Of course I do."\
"Yeah. I know. I just hope things can stay that way for you."

Before I could work out what she meant by that, the teacher's voice cut across the classroom.

"Nanase, Takamiya — no talking."\
"Yes, sir..."

Shiori gave me a wry smile and a small wink, then turned back to face the front.\
I opened my textbook, feeling something I couldn't quite name.

─────────────

After school.\
The moment class ended, I grabbed my bag and headed for Class 2's room.\
Going to meet Honoka was only natural.\
When I slid the door open, she was surrounded by classmates. Of course she was.

"Honoka."

I called her name without hesitation.\
The people around her went quiet for a moment.

"Yuna..."

Honoka looked at me, something uneasy in her expression.

"We're going home."

I took her hand.

"Oh, but..."\
"Come on."

I could feel the eyes on us. It didn't matter.

"See you, Shiraishi-san," someone called out.\
"Yeah... see you tomorrow."

Honoka smiled apologetically.

*— I hate that smile being turned on anyone who isn't me.*

"...Hey, Honoka."

Once we were out of the classroom, I squeezed her hand tight.

"What's wrong?"\
"Look at me more. Just me."

Her expression froze for a second.

"Yuna..."\
"You're my girlfriend, aren't you? But you were being so friendly with all of them..."\
"But that's just..."\
"Look at me. Care about me. Only me."

I pressed myself against her arm, holding on tight.\
Honoka looked at me, uncertain.

"Yuna... that's..."\
"I love you, Honoka."

I pulled her into a hug.

"...I love you too, Yuna."\
"Then look at me. Only me."

I gripped her hand harder.

"...Yuna, can you give me just a little more freedom?"

I went still.

"...What do you mean?"\
"I love you so much. But sometimes... I want time to talk with people, to be around others too."\
"...No."

The word came out immediately.

"...Yuna."\
"You're all I need. Is it different for you?"\
"That's not what I mean. It's just..."

Honoka let out a slow breath, like she was choosing her words with care.

"I want to be there for you. But just a little... I want you to think about it a little more."

Something snagged on those words, deep inside me.

"...I don't want to think about it."

I murmured it softly.\
Honoka gently stroked my hand.

"I love you, Yuna."

Those words, at least, I want to believe are true.\
But I was afraid.\
*If things kept going like this — I felt like Honoka was going to slip away from me.*
