**Episode 31: Unexpected — Shiori's Side**

\
*side.Shiori*

I make my way quietly back to my seat.
I set my textbook on the desk and flip through the pages at random, but none of it reaches my head at all.

*(...Why.)*

Yuna-chan put distance between herself and Shiraishi-san, once.
They really were apart, back then. I was sure of it.

— And yet.

I saw the mark on Yuna-chan's throat.
To be touched deeply enough, this early in the morning, to leave something like that behind—

*(...impossible.)*

Without meaning to, I grind my back teeth.

My read on this was completely wrong.

Shiraishi-san has a sociable personality.
She can talk to anyone in the class, always mindful of the people around her.
That was exactly why I thought she'd said she wanted "a little freedom."

I assumed she found Yuna-chan's possessiveness stifling, and meant to spend some time alone, or with people who weren't Yuna-chan.

— But.

*(...I was wrong.)*

If anything, instead of pulling apart — they seem more tightly bound together than before.

*(...This is infuriating.)*

I did close the distance with Yuna-chan, that day at the aquarium.
She spent time with me, and even while she looked lonely thinking about Shiraishi-san, she still smiled, still seemed to enjoy herself.

So that day, I made the leap and called her "Yuna-chan."
And Yuna-chan accepted it.

*(I thought I was fading Shiraishi-san's shadow, little by little.)*

Shiraishi-san's presence inside Yuna-chan is still this strong.

I clench my fist in my lap.
Quietly, so no one notices, I tighten my grip.

*(Shiraishi-san really is unfair.)*

Saying "I want freedom" of her own accord — and still managing to tie Yuna-chan to her this tightly.
And Yuna-chan, letting herself be tied down like that, too—

*(...Am I not enough?)*

The words rise up in my throat.
But I swallow them back down and let out a quiet breath.

*(...Well. Fine.)*

This isn't over yet. If anything, this is where it really begins.

I slowly lift my face.

Yuna-chan still isn't looking my way.
Her head is full of nothing but Shiraishi-san.

*(That's fine. It doesn't matter.)*

If Yuna-chan loves Shiraishi-san — then I'll just take all of it, together, as one.

I don't need her to fall for me right away.
If I can take "Yuna-chan who loves Shiraishi-san" — whole, as she is — that's fine too.

*(I want Yuna-chan.)*

However it has to happen. By whatever means necessary.

*(So I won't rush.)*

The anxiety and wavering that being with Shiraishi-san creates in Yuna-chan — I just need to slowly, patiently reel that in.

If I do, Yuna-chan will realise.

Who it is that truly fulfils her.
Who it is that should truly be by her side.

*(I'll be the one to teach her that answer.)*

If Yuna-chan doesn't notice on her own, I'll make her notice.
Whether the person beside her is really the right one. Who could truly treasure Yuna-chan the most.

Right now, maybe all she can see is Shiraishi-san.
But people's feelings change.
Little by little. Slowly. Surely.

— And besides.

*(Loving each other doesn't mean you get to stay together forever.)*

In love, feelings alone don't make everything work out.
However strongly you feel for someone, if there's enough misalignment, anxiety, doubt — the relationship grows fragile on its own.

And in fact, Yuna-chan already wavered once.

*(Which means—)*

A doubt has already taken root in Yuna-chan, about staying with Shiraishi-san forever.

That's the opening I'll exploit.

"Even if two people love each other, there are always ways to pull them apart..."

My voice was quiet.
A whisper too small for anyone to hear.

— Love alone can't keep two people together forever.

When Yuna-chan realises that, what will she do?
That's when I'll reach out my hand, gently.

*(It's okay. I'm here.)*

Yuna-chan will surely take that hand.
Let go of Shiraishi-san's, and reach for mine instead—
