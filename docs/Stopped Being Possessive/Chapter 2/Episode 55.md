**Episode 55: Quiet Time**

\
Her breathing had settled a little now, quiet and even in sleep.

Honoka was sound asleep against my chest.\
Traces of tears still clung to her cheeks, and a faint redness lingered beneath her eyes.\
Having made her cry this much — it pricked, sharp and aching, somewhere deep in my chest.

*(I'm sorry. I'm really sorry.)*

Apologizing over and over in my heart, I gently stroked her hair.\
The feel of it was so dear to me that I kept doing it, again and again, as if confirming a warmth I'd almost lost.

Time passed quietly by.

Part of me wanted to stay like this forever, but the thought crossed my mind that I should probably start making dinner soon.\
Honoka's condition worried me too — I wanted to make her something warm.

"...Sorry, just for a bit."

I whispered it quietly, and shifted my position, slowly.\
Careful not to pull Honoka's head away from my chest too suddenly, I eased her down, gently.\
Quiet, careful.

I laid her head softly onto my lap.\
At the weight of it against my thigh, I could feel Honoka's warmth seeping through, slow and steady.\
She didn't stir, her brow relaxing as if at ease, her breathing settling into a soft, even rhythm.

Watching her like that, I smiled, just a little.\
If simply being here beside her, without saying anything, was enough to bring her peace —\
that alone felt like enough.

But meaning to step away, just for a moment, I started to sit up.\
Trying carefully not to wake her, I began easing her head off my lap —

"...No... don't go..."

A thin, faint voice came from below me.

Startled, I looked down, and Honoka had her eyes half open, gripping my wrist.\
Her grip was weak, but desperate. As if she thought I might disappear somewhere again.

"Yuna... you said you wouldn't go anywhere... didn't you..."

Her voice was sleepy, and trembling faintly.\
I gently held her hand back and knelt to meet her eyes.

"I'm sorry. I was just thinking I'd make dinner..."

At that, Honoka pressed her lips together and looked down, averting her gaze.

"...You don't have to make anything... right now... I just want you beside me..."

Those words squeezed tight around my heart.\
Anxious, lonely, having trembled alone for so long — her feelings came through, painfully clear.

I sat back down beside her, quietly, and wrapped her hand in both of mine.

"Okay. I won't go anywhere. I'll stay right here."

At that, Honoka let out a small breath and leaned against my shoulder.\
Still drowsy, her body unsteady, she rested her weight against me.

Feeling her warmth, I kept gently stroking Honoka's hair.
