## *"Love Spelled Out in Woman"*

\
She clings to her, holds her — a middle schooler — as if grasping for something to keep her from falling.\
Each time she traces a body that seems distilled from pure nectar, each time she takes it into her mouth, she is swallowed by a torrent of scalding water so intense she feels she might drown in it. Her dignity and reason snap like the crude limbs of a scarecrow, and hang there, wrecked. That they never quite tear free — never quite vanish into the current — makes it all the more pitiful.\
Self-reproach is pressed always against her forehead, and the faces of her family and the face before her drift through her field of vision at random. That a day would come when the faces of her beloved husband and daughter would feel like an intrusion — she had truly, not once, imagined it. It was entirely beyond the reach of her imagination. And yet here she was, having stepped off the edge of that unimaginable place, and fallen, and was still falling.\
She catches the outstretched hand of a girl in middle school — the same age as her own daughter — and winds their fingers together, refusing to let her touch anyone else; and at that, the girl's lips relax, as if reading her intent — as if mocking it. She knows: make enough of a fool of yourself reaching for her, and the girl will only want you more. She was right. The moment their mouths meet, the other's tongue, cooler than hers, pushes past her lips, impatient, unable to wait. While the difference in temperature sends something like a shiver across her skin, that foreign tongue moves through her like a leisurely stroll. Growing conscious of the other's face, so close, as her tongue wanders — she understands: every garden her life has ever cultivated is being withered away, and in its place, something unrecognisable is being put down to grow.\
It sprouts in an instant, colours everything, changes even the shape of the sky, and transforms her inner landscape entirely.\
That landscape held within it the rain falling endlessly over a forest — and yet it was blue. And beautiful.\
When she winds her own tongue around the other's as it moves to wander off and leave — as if pressing herself against it — a thick, viscous sound is born between them, and her cheeks burn. The sound of shameless desire cuts across the edge of her ear; by the time even that pain has dissolved away, it has already become pleasure. Their tongues seeking each other out — tenderly, almost pitifully so.\
When the kiss ends, she cups a hand against her cheek — white as white, the kind that would hold a fingerprint — and feels, with relief, that it is properly flushed. Sensing something in the softening of her own face, the girl tries to look away, as if disliking that she has been seen through; she stops her with her other hand as well. Trapped with no choice but to face her directly, those eyes — as they have on countless occasions — pierce straight through her now too.\
"You dirty old lady."\
Everything that has passed through her, compressed with uncanny precision into just that much.\
"...You're hardly one to talk, you little pervert."\
Even if she were to enumerate everything this girl has done to her and throw it back — this girl would only laugh, she's sure.\
"Lucky you. That I like someone like that."\
She emphasises the detachment deliberately, putting distance between herself and what she feels.\
Always, always like this — only ever able to reach anything by going the long way around, in crooked, roundabout words.\
This girl.\
The depths of her stomach boil with it — how dear she is.\
"You too... you only ever... want me..."\
She brushes hair from her forehead, licks away the sweat rising there, pulls the body pressing down on her even closer.\
*Only ever wants me.*\
*Only me is enough.*\
*No one else. Anyone but me — unnecessary.*\
Arms reach and draw her close, and breath is blown softly against her ear.\
"Only you."\
That single reply bites into her ear. Hard, as if refusing to let go — not until it tears away.\
She wants to wreck her completely.\
She wants to hold her, gently, wrapped safe.\
That love and desire can both burn so hot they sharpen to a point, and begin to take on the same shape — what irony is that.\
*I can, if it's with you* — what she felt when those words were spoken, she will never forget, not for the rest of her life.\
To put it in commonplace terms: it was a blessing. And it was a curse.

If, in that moment, she had not — as naturally as breathing — let her maternal instincts show.\
Perhaps none of this would have happened.
